Muhammad Ali Jinnah

The founder of Pakistan
Architect of a Nation, Beacon of Unity.

Introduction

Muhammad Ali Jinnah, known as the "Quaid-e-Azam," was the visionary founder of Pakistan. His resolute commitment to democracy, religious freedom, and social justice shaped a new nation.
Explore the enduring legacy of a leader who forged Pakistan as a homeland for Muslims.

Early Life and Background

Muhammad Ali Jinnah, born in Karachi in 1876, came from a modest background. Educated in London, he became a barrister and a successful lawyer in Bombay. Jinnah's early exposure to diverse cultures influenced his inclusive worldview.
His legal career and commitment to justice laid the foundation for his pivotal role in the creation of Pakistan.

Important Dates

  • December 25, 1876

    Birth of Muhammad Ali Jinnah

    Jinnah was born in Karachi, British India, into a modest family.

  • 1892-1896

    Educational Pursuits in London

    Jinnah sailed to London for further education and studied law at the University of London. He was called to the bar in 1896.

  • 1896

    Return to India

    Jinnah returned to India and began his legal career in Bombay, where he gained recognition for his legal acumen.

  • 1906

    Entry into Politics

    Jinnah joined the Indian National Congress and embarked on his political career, advocating for Indian rights within the British system.

  • 1916

    Lucknow Session of the Congress

    Jinnah played a pivotal role in the Lucknow Session, where the Congress and the Muslim League forged the Lucknow Pact, presenting a united front against British colonial rule.

  • 1934

    Leadership of the Muslim League

    Jinnah assumed leadership of the All-India Muslim League, a pivotal moment in his political career.

  • 1940

    Lahore Resolution

    Jinnah presented the Lahore Resolution, demanding a separate Muslim state, which laid the foundation for Pakistan.

  • August 14, 1947

    Independence of Pakistan

    Pakistan was officially established, and Muhammad Ali Jinnah became its first Governor-General.

  • September 11, 1948

    Death of Jinnah

    Muhammad Ali Jinnah passed away, leaving behind a legacy as the "Father of the Nation" and founder of Pakistan.
